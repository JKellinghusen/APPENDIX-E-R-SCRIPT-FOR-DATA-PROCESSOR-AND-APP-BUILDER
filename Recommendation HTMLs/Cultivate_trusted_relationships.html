Cultivate trusted relationships

Why it matters

Trust is the foundation of strong workplace relationships and a high-performing organization. Employees who trust their colleagues and leadership are more likely to collaborate effectively, share ideas openly, and engage in problem-solving. Trust also boosts performance, reduces errors, and improves health.

A workplace built on trust leads to higher levels of psychological safety, stronger teamwork, and increased resilience during times of change. Employees who feel trusted and empowered tend to take greater ownership of their work and demonstrate higher levels of accountability.

Trust is a key ingredient to psychological safety, which boosts performance, reduces errors, and improves staff health. Employees at high-trust organizations report 74% less stress, 13% fewer sick days, 106% more energy at work, 76% more engagement, 50% higher productivity, 29% more satisfaction with their lives, and 40% less burnout than employees at low-trust organizations.

Recommendations

Earn your employees’ trust

Be honest and transparent with your staff by sharing important information, listening to employee concerns, and providing honest feedback. Demonstrate that your employees can trust you by leading with integrity and consistency on a daily basis and creating a psychologically safe environment. Changes to the company or department should not come as a surprise, especially when they affect your employees’ work or income. Include workers in discussions where possible.

Trust your staff

Much like respect, trust is a two-way street and leaders must trust their employees to deliver on their responsibilities. This is especially crucial as remote work becomes ever more common. Instead of micromanaging, setting clear and achievable goals gives employees the opportunity to rise to the challenge and complete the work in their own way.
