Foster collaboration and teamwork

Why it matters

Leaders of aid organizations will understand the value of teamwork and collaboration in aid work. Workplaces that promote these traits see better program outcomes, improved problem solving, reduced burnout, and higher individual performance. Intra-team collaboration sets the foundation for inter-organizational collaboration, which has been shown to make humanitarian response more effective and efficient.

Recommendations

Build successful teams

Select people with the right skills to achieve the organizationâ€™s goal. If they have not worked together before, give them an easily achievable goal early on to build confidence and camaraderie.

Establish clear roles and responsibilities among team members to avoid confusion and competition for leadership. Make it clear that the success of the team reflects on each member and vice versa in order to promote a supportive environment and foster trust.

Utilize proven team development interventions (TDIs) such as team training, leadership training, team building, and team debriefing.

Enable collaboration for solo workers

Remote workers or employees who do not have a dedicated team or department can still benefit from the productivity and wellness boost that collaborative work provides. Find ways to include them in interdepartmental projects or get them involved in initiatives outside of their daily responsibilities, such as ERGs or social workplace activities.

For mixed teams of in-person and remote workers, make sure that remote workers and not excluded. For example, rather than holding meetings with part of the team in a conference room and some people on video call, consider making the meeting completely virtual to put everyone on the same level.

Some people prefer to work alone, so if their job does not require teamwork, forcing them to join a team will not benefit them or the organization. Instead, provide regular opportunities to join collaborative activities.
