Connect individual work with organizational mission

Why it matters 

In aid organizations, it is easy to assume that all employees are driven by the mission. Many employees are directly connected to the mission – providing services to beneficiaries and seeing the outcomes of their work on a daily basis – while others, such as administrative staff, are more removed. But how many of them actually know your mission statement? Even though most aid worker have an inherent sense of altruism, they can still benefit from intentional efforts to reinforce your organization’s mission and connect it to their daily work.

A strong connection between individual work and an organization’s mission fosters motivation, engagement, and a sense of purpose. Employees who understand how their contributions align with broader organizational goals experience greater job satisfaction and commitment.

Shared purpose within an organization also enhances collaboration across teams. When employees see how different roles contribute to a collective objective, silos break down, and cooperation improves.

 

Recommendations

Create or review your mission statement

Do you have a well-crafted mission statement, honed over years of meetings, discussions, and consultations? Or have you not really gotten around to creating one yet? Either way, when you create or review the statement, make sure it reflects the current goals and values of the organization and is cognizant of the current state of the world.

Communicate the mission clearly and consistently

Once you have a mission statement make sure that your employees know it, internalize it, and practice it. One way to do this is to flesh it out and make it actionable by creating a Values and Behaviors framework.

To reinforce the mission, values, and behaviors, make them omnipresent throughout the organization:

-          Add them to documents, email signatures, posters.

-          Keep them top of mind by framing strategic meetings and program planning activities around them – ensuring that the organization’s actions are in line with its mission.

-          Include them in performance reviews to measure how each employee is contributing to them.

-          Integrate them into onboarding and training programs.

-          Celebrate mission-related achievements casually or formally

Share mission-related achievements

While many frontline workers see the how their work contributes to the mission directly, admin staff and others who are more removed from the programs may lack that critical feedback. Sharing success stories from the field, testimonials from beneficiaries, or reports on program outcomes can help employees realize how their work connects to the mission. Whenever possible, take time to highlight how logistical, administrative, or technical roles contribute to life-changing interventions to help all staff members feel connected to the mission.
