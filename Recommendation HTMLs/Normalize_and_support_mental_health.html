Normalize and support mental health

Why it matters

Mental ill-health and acute mental illness are unfortunately prevalent among aid organizations, where experiences of trauma and chronic stress are common and risk of PTSD, depression and anxiety is elevated compared to among the general population. Every aspect of the Workplace Wellbeing Assessment relates back to mental health and wellbeing, but this section hones in on preventing, combating, or even living with mental health conditions, as well as addressing the associated stigma.

Like an infection, untreated mental health concerns can grow into complex and dangerous illness. At the very least, this can reduce productivity and effectiveness. In the worst case, untreated mental illness can lead someone to take their own life.

Therefore, it is crucial for organizations to make mental health support readily available and to combat stigma, normalizing discussions of mental health, encouraging employees to seek help when they need it, and creating a psychologically safe environment that discourages employees from repressing or hiding mental health conditions.

Recommendations

Ensure access to mental health resources

Provide free or subsidized mental health resources to all employees via benefits packages, including access to an in-house or independent counselor. Create fact sheets or guides for supervisors and employees to provide quick information on mental health services.

Combat the stigma of seeking help

Encourage open discussions about mental health through awareness campaigns, workshops, and peer support programs. Fostering open dialogue about mental health reduces stigma and increases help-seeking behavior.

While all employees should feel comfortable bringing up mental health concerns, personal and cultural factors may prevent some people from doing so. Therefore, it is important to also provide routes for employees to seek support anonymously, such as anonymous chats.

Protect employees with mental health conditions

In many countries, mental illness is protected under disability laws. Nevertheless, let employees know that being diagnosed does not have to affect their job. Incorporate language into organizational policies to guarantee reasonable accommodations for mental health conditions.

Integrate mental health into the organizational policies

Create and promote Employee Assistance Programs to provide critical services.

Hold annual trainings on peer support methods and recognizing suicide warning signs
